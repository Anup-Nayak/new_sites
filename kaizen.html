Rakshin Workshop (Anti-Child Abuse)

The Rakshin Workshop aimed to raise awareness about child sex abuse among college students and promote open conversations on the topic. Participants learned about the POSCO Act, recognizing signs of abuse, redressal and prevention mechanisms, and the importance of consent. The event saw active engagement and interaction, primarily due to the enthusiastic participation of students from other colleges. While children from IIT Delhi remained silent, the workshop's success was credited to the engaging prime speaker, the Director of Rakshin Project, and the receptive attitude of non-IIT Delhi students. Representatives from Sakshi NGO were cooperative, further contributing to the workshop's positive outcome. The event exemplified the potential impact of informed discussions and experienced speakers in spreading awareness about child sex abuse, fostering a safer environment for children, and empowering students to advocate for a safer society.

Seva Summit: The social symposium

The Seva Summit held on April 1, 2023, brought together NGOs and corporate social responsibility (CSR) teams, providing a platform for NGOs to showcase their initiatives and visions. They presented an overview of their past and ongoing projects, highlighting positive outcomes achieved through their efforts. The event aimed to foster collaboration between NGOs and CSR teams by addressing specific challenges faced by NGOs and exploring sustainable solutions. It facilitated meaningful interactions, enabling both parties to identify areas of alignment for achieving common social service goals. The summit allowed NGOs to interact with CSR teams, potentially leading to fruitful partnerships and collective impact in societal upliftment. The participation of nine NGOs showcasing their work through presentations added value to the event, recognizing their exceptional contributions. The Seva Summit emphasized the power of collaboration in driving positive social change and promoting impactful initiatives within the community.

Sociopreneur Samvaad

Sociopreneur Samvaad featured esteemed socially focused entrepreneurs, including Sharad Vivek Sagar, Tejas Sidnal, and Susmith C. Suseelan, engaging in panel discussions. The event attracted a good turnout, and the audience actively participated, posing insightful questions during the discussions. The moderator skillfully conducted the event, displaying thorough research and maintaining consistency throughout. Sociopreneur Samvaad provided a valuable platform for attendees to gain insights from successful entrepreneurs in the social sector, fostering meaningful discussions and knowledge-sharing. The focus on socially focused entrepreneurship inspired the audience with real-life success stories, instilling a culture of innovation and impact-driven initiatives within the community. The event exemplified the potential of entrepreneurship in driving positive social change, encouraging attendees to embark on impactful ventures for societal well-being.

Yuva chetna

Yuva Chetna, a Panel Discussion on Youth Engagement in Social Work, featured distinguished IAS officers Anil Swaroop and Dr. Hukum Singh Meena, IAS Additional Secretary, Department of Land Resources, Ministry of Rural Development. The event provided a platform to gain firsthand insights into the immense value of social work and deepened attendees' knowledge in this must-attend event. The panel discussion was highly interactive, with people staying even after the event to listen to the guests. For aspiring civil servants, the event proved valuable as they gained insights into the long-term aspects of the job. Yuva Chetna exemplified the importance of engaging youth in social work and provided a motivating experience for attendees, encouraging them to contribute actively to the betterment of society.

Street art

IIT Delhi organized a Street Art Competition on the SAC Lawns, attracting teams from colleges in and around Delhi. The theme focused on the social domain, inspiring participants to express creativity and messages through art. The vibrant artwork created an impactful atmosphere on campus. The event received recognition beyond the campus and was featured in "The Times of India" newspaper. It highlighted the event's significance in promoting artistic expression and social awareness. By encouraging talented artists to address social issues, the competition showcased art's power in spreading important messages. It exemplified IIT Delhi's commitment to fostering creativity and social consciousness among students and the community.
