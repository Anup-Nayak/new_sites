Know Your Rights Campaign

The "Know Your Rights" (KYR) initiative at IIT Delhi aimed to educate marginalized communities about government policies. As part of this mission, a campus-wide booklet distribution campaign was organized to enlighten IIT Delhi's security and housing staff about relevant government policies that could improve their work conditions. The campaign took place in two phases, targeting security personnel in the Biotech grounds on September 4th, and housing workers behind the Mittal Complex on September 6th. Volunteers' disciplined and punctual approach, along with booklets printed in Hindi, contributed to the campaign's success. The initiative received enthusiastic participation from the staff, fostering a receptive environment for knowledge sharing. The well-executed design and timely delivery of quality booklets further enhanced their impact, empowering the recipients with valuable information about their rights under government policies.

Talk Vegan Event

NSS IITD organized the "Talk Vegan" event, featuring a screening of the documentary "The Land of Ahimsa" by Climate Healers, followed by a panel discussion on "Ushering a Global Shift towards Plant-Based Systems" in collaboration with FIAPO. The event highlighted the positive impact of a vegan lifestyle in preventing animal suffering and reducing the environmental footprint, particularly in addressing climate change. Attendees had the opportunity to interact with influential personalities like Dolly Vyas-Ahuja, an Ahimsa Animal Activist and Documentary Producer, and Dr. Sailesh Rao, Founder and Executive Director of Climate Healers. The engaging panel discussion received positive responses, demonstrating attendees' active participation and interest. The event successfully delivered valuable knowledge about veganism, leaving participants informed and inspired to promote plant-based systems for a more sustainable and compassionate future.

Aarohan

Aarohan is an educational project aimed at assisting 11th and 12th-grade students with their competitive exam preparations and board exams. The project provides quality education and comprehensive bilingual assignments on various topics to support the students' learning journey. Additionally, Aarohan promotes adult literacy by offering spoken English classes to the staff members of IIT Delhi. In its initial phase, Aarohan focused on creating assignments for JEE Mains/Boards and JEE Advanced. Around 50 assignments were completed by re-recruiting old volunteers before regular volunteering for Phase 1 began. In the first semester, Phase 1 volunteering was conducted to further support the students' exam preparations. During the course of the project, Aarohan expanded its impact by offering spoken English classes in collaboration with the EdJustice NGO. This initiative witnessed over 60 regular classes, benefiting students and enhancing their English language skills, which resulted in some students excelling in their entrance examinations. The project also successfully designed the curriculum for spoken English classes, secured the permission of IIT Delhi's director for conducting the classes, and collaborated with various NGOs in Delhi for offline classes. Aarohan's achievements include the preparation of more than 180 assignments, the successful conduct of over 60 classes with regular attendance, and the positive impact on students' academic performance. The project's dedication to quality education and adult literacy showcases its commitment to making a meaningful difference in the lives of the students and staff members at IIT Delhi.

Animal Care

The Animal Care project is dedicated to promoting animal welfare and raising awareness about the well-being of animals. The project focuses on sensitizing people towards animals and fostering empathy towards them. To achieve its goals, the project facilitates interactions between people and animals both on and off campus, provides guidance on how to interact with animals, and offers a platform for animal enthusiasts to actively participate in animal welfare initiatives. The project's work includes monitoring the regular feeding of dogs by guards at various locations on campus, restructuring the treatment of animal injuries, and creating a database of vets near the campus for quick access to medical assistance. Volunteers have actively taken care of injured or sick dogs, cats, and birds by providing necessary medical attention. A trip to the Friendicoes animal shelter was organized, and an animal first-aid workshop was conducted to educate volunteers on providing initial care to injured animals. The project's achievements include obtaining permission from ADHM to collect food from hostels around the campus for feeding animals, organizing a successful animal care trip to Friendicoes, Gurugram, and conducting a workshop on Animal First Aid in collaboration with STRAW and FIAPPO NGOs. Additionally, the project has ensured the well-being of campus dogs during winters by providing them with mattresses and jackets for protection from the cold. The Animal Care project's dedication to promoting animal welfare and creating a compassionate environment on the campus reflects its commitment to making a positive impact on the lives of animals and inspiring others to actively contribute to their well-being.

Ankuram

It provides conversational English and basic literacy skills to economically disadvantaged adult women, equipping them with valuable knowledge and abilities. Collaborating with organizations like VIDYA NGO Gurgaon, KV VIDYA, and Prayas Juvenile Center, the project has conducted regular offline classes for Vidya beneficiaries and taught basic English to over 20 women. The successful collaboration with Prayas NGO has enabled teaching sessions for girls who have faced challenging circumstances. Ankuram's dedication to empowering women through education and fostering collaborations reflects its strong commitment to making a positive impact on adult literacy and women's empowerment in India.

Jyoti

Project Jyoti is a dedicated initiative aimed at empowering visually impaired students by providing them with personal, academic, and emotional assistance to unleash their true potential. Through regular volunteering sessions at NAB RK Puram and NAB Hauz Khas, volunteers offered academic support to students in classes 9th to 12th, fostering a nurturing learning environment. The project's commitment to inclusivity extended beyond the classroom, as it facilitated scribes for recruitment and admission exams, ensuring equal opportunities for visually impaired individuals in prestigious government agencies and colleges. Additionally, Project Jyoti provided special teaching assistance to students from different colleges and organizations in Delhi NCR, promoting inclusivity and equal access to education. By organizing Ujjwala Fest at NAB RK Puram, the project fostered an inclusive and vibrant atmosphere, celebrating the unique talents and potential of visually impaired students. Through personalized assistance to a working professional during her departmental examination, the project demonstrated its dedication to empowering individuals and promoting independence. With its impactful initiatives and collaborations, Project Jyoti has made a significant difference in the lives of visually impaired students, empowering them to realize their dreams and aspirations.

Munirka

Project Munirka is an impactful initiative that collaborates with NGOs Samadhan and Sahyog to provide educational support to students in classes 4th to 10th. With a focus on subjects like Maths, English, Science, and Computer, volunteers conduct regular classes for students, nurturing their academic growth and providing additional guidance beyond school teachings. The project's collaboration with Sahyog, a new NGO active in Munirka, expands its reach and impact, ensuring more students receive the support they need. Classes at Samadhan are conducted in two slots for grades 9 and 10, with subjects like Science, Maths, and English being covered from Monday to Friday, while computer classes are held every Sunday for students in grades 7 to 10. With dedicated volunteers and continuous communication with the POCs of the NGOs, Project Munirka creates a positive learning environment for students, helping them strengthen their fundamentals and gain holistic knowledge across diverse subjects. The initiative's commitment to empowering students and promoting educational excellence makes it a significant contributor to the community in Munirka.

Old age home

The Old Age Home Project is dedicated to providing mental support and affection to elderly residents living in old age homes. The project's main objective is to raise awareness among students about the significance of elderly individuals in our lives and the joy of bringing smiles to their faces through spending quality time with them. The initiative involves organizing various recreational activities and interactive sessions to engage and uplift the spirits of the elderly. Work done by the project includes the "Letters of Hope" activity, where 10+ volunteers co-managed the writing of poems to provide emotional support to the beneficiaries in the old age homes. The heartfelt letters were personally handed over to the residents, leading to joy and happiness among them. Additionally, the project visited the Panchvati Maidan Old Age Home, where volunteers had enriching conversations with an elderly lady and explored opportunities for future collaborations. The project also celebrated Grandparents' Day with the NSS team, conducting music performances, jamming sessions, Shayri, and dancing, creating a delightful experience for all the residents. The Old Age Home Project's compassionate efforts to bring happiness and companionship to the elderly demonstrate its commitment to making a positive impact in their lives and fostering a sense of empathy and respect among the student community.

Social Media

Vidya

The VIDYA Teaching Project is a valuable collaboration with VIDYA NGO, where a dedicated team of volunteers conducts regular classes for students of classes 10th and 12th, focusing on subjects like Maths, Physics, Chemistry, Data Entry, Computer, and Web Designing at Kendriya Vidyalaya School on the IIT Delhi Campus. The classes are held on weekdays from 3-5 pm, and the volunteers provide comprehensive academic support through regular classes, assignments, fun activities, and assessment exams. The project's work includes conducting educational trips, hosting awareness events, and promoting mental health activities for the students in collaboration with other NSS projects. The project smoothly transitioned from online to offline mode during the tenure and also extended its outreach by providing teaching assistance to students in Panchsheel Park when KV was closed. Volunteers went above and beyond to provide personalized support by preparing class notes based on the NIOS syllabus in two levels of difficulty. Additionally, on Teachers' Day, the project brought motivation and inspiration to the students through a special session with Samrat sir. Overall, the VIDYA Teaching Project played a crucial role in empowering underprivileged students by providing them with quality education, support, and encouragement, creating a positive impact on their academic journey and fostering a brighter future for them.

Vriddhi

Project Vriddhi is a collaborative initiative with the NGO Food 4 Thought, dedicated to providing English classes to students in grades 2 to 8. Held once a week on Saturdays, the project aims to enhance the students' English-speaking abilities and overall language proficiency. Volunteers play a pivotal role in this endeavor, engaging the children in reading and reciting storybooks and poems, fostering a love for language and literature. To ensure the effectiveness of the classes, the project conducted two orientation sessions to acquaint volunteers with Vriddhi's goals and mission. The recruitment of enthusiastic volunteers further enriched the project's impact, leading to the successful organization of over 10 engaging offline classes with the young learners. Beyond the regular classes, Project Vriddhi ventured into diverse activities to create a holistic learning experience. A notable field trip to Amrit Udayan saw the participation of more than 50 NSS volunteers, fostering a sense of community and making a positive impact on both the volunteers and the children. Additionally, the project encouraged volunteers to delve into governmental policies through informative presentations, expanding their knowledge beyond the classroom walls. The project's achievements extend beyond imparting language skills; it succeeded in creating an enjoyable and enriching learning environment where students eagerly embraced their education journey. The beaming smiles on the faces of the children, coupled with the volunteers' joy in teaching, exemplify the project's meaningful impact. Project Vriddhi exemplifies the essence of the NSS community's commitment to empowering young minds, nurturing a love for learning, and making a positive difference in the lives of those they touch.
