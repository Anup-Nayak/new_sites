Know Your Rights Campaign

The "Know Your Rights" (KYR) initiative at IIT Delhi aimed to educate marginalized communities about government policies. As part of this mission, a campus-wide booklet distribution campaign was organized to enlighten IIT Delhi's security and housing staff about relevant government policies that could improve their work conditions. The campaign took place in two phases, targeting security personnel in the Biotech grounds on September 4th, and housing workers behind the Mittal Complex on September 6th. Volunteers' disciplined and punctual approach, along with booklets printed in Hindi, contributed to the campaign's success. The initiative received enthusiastic participation from the staff, fostering a receptive environment for knowledge sharing. The well-executed design and timely delivery of quality booklets further enhanced their impact, empowering the recipients with valuable information about their rights under government policies.

Menstrual Hygiene Workshop

The Menstrual Hygiene Workshop aimed to raise awareness about menstrual hygiene and sanitation practices prevalent in rural areas. The event shed light on existing taboos and practices that could lead to health issues, emphasizing the need for effective solutions. Despite being a female-centric topic, the workshop received active participation from both male and female attendees, fostering an inclusive environment. Valuable insights were gained, revealing the gravity of the issue in rural communities. The workshop demonstrated IIT Delhi's commitment to promoting health awareness and addressing critical challenges faced by rural populations. By tackling menstrual hygiene-related stigmas and taboos, the initiative contributed to empowering individuals and fostering positive changes in the communities it served.

Gender Sensitization Workshop

The Gender Sensitization Workshop aimed to educate participants about the LGBTQIA+ community. In collaboration with Soch Anth Hi Aarambh NGO, the workshop featured engaging presentations by speakers from the LGBTQIA+ community, challenging stereotypes and fostering gender sensitization. Participants attentively took part in the interactive session, driven by the speakers' excellent delivery skills and their willingness to address audience questions. The well-planned workshop achieved its goal of attracting the desired number of participants, contributing to a more empathetic and inclusive environment at IIT Delhi. This joint initiative demonstrated the institute's commitment to promoting understanding and breaking barriers to create a more accepting and compassionate campus community.

Movie screening

IIT Delhi organized documentary screenings on various topics, including "Talk Vegan," "The Last Drop" (water conservation), and "Hidden Figures" (based on talented individuals facing racism). These screenings provided a platform for students to explore important social and environmental issues. "Talk Vegan" aimed to raise awareness about a vegan lifestyle's impact on animal welfare and the environment. "The Last Drop" shed light on water conservation, emphasizing the importance of preserving this vital resource. "Hidden Figures" showcased the inspiring stories of talented individuals overcoming racial discrimination. Through these documentaries, students gained valuable insights and were encouraged to engage in discussions on pressing societal challenges. IIT Delhi's initiative in hosting these documentary screenings demonstrated its commitment to promoting awareness and fostering a conscious community.

Talk Vegan Event

NSS IITD organized the "Talk Vegan" event, featuring a screening of the documentary "The Land of Ahimsa" by Climate Healers, followed by a panel discussion on "Ushering a Global Shift towards Plant-Based Systems" in collaboration with FIAPO. The event highlighted the positive impact of a vegan lifestyle in preventing animal suffering and reducing the environmental footprint, particularly in addressing climate change. Attendees had the opportunity to interact with influential personalities like Dolly Vyas-Ahuja, an Ahimsa Animal Activist and Documentary Producer, and Dr. Sailesh Rao, Founder and Executive Director of Climate Healers. The engaging panel discussion received positive responses, demonstrating attendees' active participation and interest. The event successfully delivered valuable knowledge about veganism, leaving participants informed and inspired to promote plant-based systems for a more sustainable and compassionate future.

Mardon Wali Baat Workshop

The "Mardon Wali Baat" workshop, conducted in collaboration with IGES and YP foundation, spanned two days, focusing on 'gender, masculinities, and relationships' through interactive sessions. Participants explored topics like gender identities, relationships, social media, consent, and violence in a safe and engaging environment . Positive feedback was received from both participants and resource persons . The workshop garnered decent attendance, reflecting the keen interest in these crucial topics.Volunteers found a non-judgmental space to share gender-related challenges and experiences. Lunch arrangements from the DMS canteen were well-received, ensuring comfort during the extended sessions. Engaging activities facilitated interactions, making the sessions enjoyable and impactful. Through "Mardon Wali Baat," IIT Delhi, IGES, and YP foundation demonstrated their commitment to promoting inclusive discussions on gender-related issues, fostering understanding and empathy within the campus community.

Office of Accessible Education

The Office of Accessible Education at IIT Delhi was inaugurated from 27th November to 2nd December 2022. As part of their initiatives, the office organized a series of events for people with disabilities, focusing on promoting accessibility and inclusivity. NSS played a crucial role in supporting these events by providing volunteers to assist with logistics and management. The events aimed to create a welcoming and supportive environment for individuals with disabilities, allowing them to participate and engage actively. The overall objective was to raise awareness about the importance of accessibility and foster a more inclusive campus community. Through these efforts, IIT Delhi demonstrated its commitment to ensuring equal opportunities and empowering individuals with disabilities to thrive within the academic environment.
